November 8, 2023

Reflection

Our group has had quite a struggle finding a client, between Gill and I we have probably called about 35 people. Originally our ideal client was a disabled veteran with an upper limb difference, but we have not had no success finding a disabled veteran to work with, so we have broadened our client pool to anyone with need for a prosthetic arm. This person will perform many everyday tasks over the course of a day, from picking things up, to turning a door knob. They will need a versatile prosthetic arm. We tried to find our client by calling VA offices, Disabled American Veterans, emailing the Brandeis engineer Greg for contacts, and I even reached out to a lot of the veterans service groups at the Brandeis career fair, all with no luck. Once we broadened our client pool Gill reached out to a lot of prosthesis labs, which gave him contacts, that gave him another contact, but a lot of people ultimately said they would get back to him, so hopefully it works out. We created an email template that we have largely copied when writing all our emails, but emails have not been very successful. The only meeting we have had so far was a phone call with a Brandeis alumni and marine Charles Frangis. We are hoping he can get us in touch with someone in need of a prosthesis, but mostly the meeting was helpful because he gave us some ideas of more places to look. Charles gave us a contact of his own who is a Captain in the marines, but he also gave us the idea of contacting veteran homeless shelters. We did two design methodologies, one was an empathy activity, thinking about the clients needs, and the other was thinking about potential designs in terms of feasibility versus impact. One problem we identified is that a client may have poor weight distribution between their arm and their prosthetic arm. We want to balance this out by making careful weight considerations, and we debated and fused several ways of doing that from our original five ideas which we ranked. The overwhelming amount of our time was spent making calls and sending emails. It would be great to build an open source device, but I think all of us really want to have that experience of working with a client, so we will continue to push to get a client, but if it doesn’t work out I’m sure we will design a great open source device for someone to use down the line.
